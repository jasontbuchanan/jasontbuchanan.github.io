Lines That May Fall Under Your Eye by Jason Thorpe Buchanan was composed in the winter of 2006-2007. It was commissioned by the San Jose State University Concert Choir under the direction of Prof. Julie Yu. The selection of text was a joint effort between the composer and director. The text is an excerpt from the "Dear Sarah" letter written by Major Sullivan Ballou, who was killed in the civil war only a week after writing the letter to his wife. He was 32 at the time of his death in the first Battle of Bull Run, July 21, 1861. This excerpt was chosen for its evocative message and vivid imagery, lending itself to text painting in the music through the use of roving modal harmonies, dense chord voicing, chromaticism and irregular changes in meter.

 

Rossetti Song Cycle was composed during the fall semester of 2007. The three poems were written by the English artist and poet Dante Gabriel Rossetti(1828-1882). He was probably better known for his paintings than his poetry, though he fared well in both mediums. The three poems deal with similar themes and concepts, while the music is tied together through the use of similar harmonic material, textures and rhythmic material.

 

Etude for Solo Violin was composed during the spring semester of 2006, intended to be a pedagogical work not only for the performer but the composer as well. It was written without accompaniment, which exposes the soloist and requires the instrument to provide it's own harmony. The injection of chromatic tones into the modal context can at first be unsettling, but the logic behind the construction of the melodic lines soon becomes apparent.

 

Amorphous Recitations was composed during the spring semester of 2006, and is a single movement work for String Quartet. The reoccurring theme is shifted chromatically and reshaped throughout the composition, accompanied by a contrasting theme made up of quartal and quintal harmonies. The music is not intended to be "programmatic," but rather to be interpreted by the mind of the listener, where it may take whatever form is desired.

 

Crosswinds was composed during the fall semester of 2005. This duet for Flute and Bb Clarinet was composed in a pandiatonic style. The quasi-functional harmonies are implied through the thin textures of the piece, enhanced by the contrasting colors provided by the two woodwinds. The work is almost entirely through composed, with the exception of the sparse return of the opening theme.
